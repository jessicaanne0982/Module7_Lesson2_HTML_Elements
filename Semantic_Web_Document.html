My Mission to make Gluten Free Delicious

About 8 years ago, I was diagnosed with a gluten allergy, and it completely turned my world upside down. I love all things baked, but my new diagnosis meant I'd have to have to find alternatives if I wanted to continue enjoying yummy baked goods. It was at that time that I started my mission to bake gluten free goodies that tasted better than their "gluten-full" counterparts.

What is a Gluten Free Diet

Being gluten free means no longer eating anything containing gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, or rye.

This includes avoiding foods such as:

  • Breads & pastries
  • Crackers & Pretzels
  • Breaded foods
  • Cereals
  • Pasta
  • Many other hidden sources of wheat, barley, or rye and their derivitives

Baking Gluten Free

After much trial and error, I quickly learned that good gluten free baking comes down to the ingredients you use, ESPECIALLY the flour. A good flour blend makes all the difference in the world when you're baking gluten free. Over the years, I have learned which blends give me the results I want and which leave me with gritty, inedible concoctions. I also invest in highest quality ingredients possible - Madagascar vanilla, Ghirardelli chocolates, and only full fat butters, creams, and milks.

My 2 favorite types of flours to use are King Arthur's Measure-for-Measure and Bob's Red Mill 1-to-1 Baking Flour.

Baking is my love language

These days, I bake almost every weekend, whether it's for my family or surprise parties or for those simply in need of a smile. Baking brings me joy, and I just love to see the smile on people's faces when they eat one of my baked goods.

Since my mission began, I've surprised many "gluten eaters" with my baked goods. Everything that leaves my kitchen now is gluten free, and I absolutely love to see the look of shock on their faces when they learn they've just eaten a gluten free treat. I often hear, "I had NO idea! That was better than a 'normal' one."
